Following a muscle-building routine offers outsized benefits for health and longevity. Increased lean mass increases insulin sensitivity, improves cardiovascular fitness, and reduces all-cause mortality risks. Additional, significant health benefits include:

  • Axial compression (i.e. lifting weights) has been shown to improve bone density markers as women age and reduce the rate of hip fractures.
  • Resistance exercise has been shown to be 150% more effective than medication or cognitive behavior therapy at improving depression, stress, and anxiety symptoms.

Target 150+ minutes per week of exercise across both:

  • Cardio (increasing your heart rate for extended periods of time): running, swimming, biking, rowing, HIIT, circuit training
  • Strength Training (and hypertrophy training): weight lifting, resistance exercise, calisthenics

Implementation:

  • Add 20 minutes of hypertrophy training per week, each week, to your routine until you're averaging 60+ minutes per week.
  • Sufficiently optimal strength and hypertrophy results can be achieved in 3 one-hour sessions per week, but a mere 60 minutes is enough to realize significant health improvements.
