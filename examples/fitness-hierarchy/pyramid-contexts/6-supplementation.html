If you are reducing calorie intake for weight loss, you will benefit from supplementing your diet with a clean source of protein (e.g. whey isolate or fermented pea protein).

Several other supplements offer wide-ranging, scientifically proven benefits:

  • Creatine: Improves both cognitive and physical performance, target 5g per day
  • Omega-3 Fats: Improves brain, cardiovascular, and mental health, target 1000mg of EPA+DHA per day

Yet other supplements can be added at your discretion or generally ignored, offering little scientific benefit

  • Greens Powders: Most offerings are nothing more than a multivitamin with fewer nutrients.
  • Multivitamin: If you're concerned you're missing key nutrients in your diet: improve your diet.
  • Hydration Mixes: Lmnt, Gatorade, etc. offer electrolytes that you likely aren't lacking.
  • Theanine, Arginine, Citrulline, GABA, CoQ10, and others have mixed results in scientific studies.

Implementation:

  • Implement a supplement testing protocol. Add one supplement at a time to your daily routine for, e.g., 30 days then remove it for 7-14 days. Assess if you notice a material difference.
  • Or don't; supplementation is optional, though creatine and Omega 3's are highly recommended.
